Jeremy's Top Ten Favorite Body Building Exercies




10. Lat Pull From Top

This type of row will hit that bottom portion of the lat. The lat is a very large muscle so you need several exercies to hit it all.

9. Seated straight leg Calf Extentions

This exercise with this machine is amazing for the calfs it gives such an amazing pump and it just feel amazing. It also doesn't load on your shoulders like a regular standing would so you wouldn't feel much in your spine.



8. Laying Hamstring curls

These are great for hitting the hammies in a lengthed postion. I also just like the way it feels better than any other hammies machine.

7. Straight Leg Deadlift

This will be very very similar to what people call RDL's but the one minor differnece is that a Straight leg will hit more hammies because you don't bend the knee where as a RDL you bend the knee which hits glutes.



6. Chest Supported T-Bar Row

This is similar to the other chest supported row but can target slightly differnet parts of the back. With the bottom grips itll line up more with the rear delt fibers and hit them good.

5. Incline Chest Press

It is an amazing exercise that lines up greatly with the upper portion of the chest. I love this exercise and it gives an amazing pump.


4. Chest Supported Row

A Chest supported row like this is an amazing exercise for say the upper back as it gives you full chest support for maximum stablity and maximum pull

3. Converging Chest Press

A converging chest press like the one in the picture is a type of chest machine that when pushed apon it will converg which is the main function of the chest so it lines up perfectly with the muscle fibers and is great for chest growth


2. Bulgarian Split Squat

A type of single leg lunge with a rear foot elevated used to work the quads in a lengthed position like the hack squat but single leg and loaded differently

1. Hack Squat

The hack squat involves standing on the plate, leaning back onto the pads at an angle, with the weight placed on top of you by positioning yourself under the shoulder pads. The weight is then pushed in the concentric phase of the squat. Simply put, when you stand back up, that's when the weight is pushed away from you.
